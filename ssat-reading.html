Passage: The Mystery of the Lost Colony

In the late 16th century, more than one hundred English settlers arrived on Roanoke Island, just off the coast of what is now North Carolina. Led by Governor John White, they hoped to establish the first permanent English colony in the New World. The settlers faced many difficulties, including food shortages, unfamiliar weather, and tense relationships with local Native American tribes.

After several months, Governor White returned to England to seek help and supplies for the struggling colony. His trip, however, was unexpectedly delayed for three years due to the outbreak of war between England and Spain. When White finally returned to Roanoke Island in 1590, he found the settlement completely deserted. The only clue left behind was the word “CROATOAN” carved into a wooden post.

The fate of the “Lost Colony” has remained one of America’s oldest mysteries. Some historians believe the settlers may have joined the nearby Croatoan tribe, while others think they perished from disease, starvation, or conflict. Despite centuries of searching, no conclusive evidence has ever been discovered to explain what happened to the colonists of Roanoke.
